Watch out for

malicious mail!

 

This training is a simulation training for phishing mail for information protection

and personal information protection awareness of Spoon Radio employees.

 

If the situation was real, your personal information could have been leaked

and your PC could have been infected with malicious code.

 

  • As malicious mail is often used as an initial attack method for large-scale damage such as
  • confidential leakage and money damage, employees are required to pay attention when receiving e-mail.

    •  

  • ■ Precautions for receiving mail ■
  • 1. Did you check the sender address?
  • You should suspect the first email address or an external email address..
    •  
  • 2. Did you check the URL address of the screen where you enter your personal information?
  • You should be careful if the URL connects to an external site or is in IP format.
    •  
  • 3. You should delete unclear or misleading mail without opening it.
    •  
  • 4. If you suspect a malicious email, you should report it to the person in charge immediately.
